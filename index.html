Research Gap

Identifying the Research Gap

1. Gevindu
Though some of the recent works have been focused on individual gem classification and material identification, not many have even tackled the issue of finding occluded gemstones in jewelry pieces, particularly in varying lighting conditions and also occlusions. Most standard methods fumble with partial visibility or misclassification when the gem is embedded in reflective metallic components. Besides, fewer studies have been done to segment gem and non-gem objects from actual images when the jewelry is worn or in its natural settings. The objective of this project is to address that gap through the reporting of a robust segmentation and classification model capable of identifying gem elements in situ, paving the way for enhanced gem analysis as well as enhanced user experiences in digital jewelry platforms.

2. Missaka
Despite the advances in gemological analysis, current methods of distinguishing real and imitation gems rely on expensive, advanced equipment (e.g., spectroscopy, X-ray fluorescence) and require experienced operators, thus out of reach for the masses and small-scale jewelers. Very little work has been done on the utilization of AI and computer vision techniques on this specific application, especially on the basis of visual data alone. Moreover, the few existing techniques suffer from classification accuracy due to the high visual similarity of some artificial and natural stones. This paper closes this gap through the development of an accessible and scalable approach by exploiting deep learning models for authenticity detection based on image-based features alone, which makes it possible for broader use in the jewelry industry.

3. Banula
Though work has been done on the use of computer vision in gem categorizing and AR-based virtual try-on solutions, current solutions do not typically integrate computerized gem shape identification with dynamic real-time 3D jewelry personalization. They typically employ manual gem shape selection or provide static 3D models that aren't dynamically altered as per the input of the user's gem. Furthermore, existing research does not address smoothly substituting gem textures on 3D models or supporting dynamic material (color) alteration in a web-based setting. This research fills the gap by combining gem shape identification, smart design suggestion, realistic gem substitution, and interactive personalization in a comprehensive, real-time system that fosters accuracy and user experience in digital jewelry visualization.

4. Vishwa
Virtual jewelry customization are rarely integrated with customized design recommendations by current jewelry industry technologies. Less-than-ideal design choices result from the inability of current virtual try-on technologies to adequately depict the true proportions of gemstones in relation to the jewelry. The virtual changing room improves accuracy and user experience even more, providing the jewelry sector with a smooth and creative solution.

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

In the evolving landscape of digital jewelry applications, there is a growing need for intelligent systems that can seamlessly analyze, classify, and personalize gemstone-based designs. However, several challenges hinder the development of a fully automated and user-adaptive solution. One major issue is the accurate identification of gemstones within complex jewelry pieces, such as rings or pendants, where gems are often partially obscured by metal settings or appear under varied lighting and orientations. Furthermore, differentiating between real and synthetic gemstones using only image data presents another challenge, as these materials often exhibit highly similar visual characteristics that require expert knowledge or specialized equipment to distinguish. Adding to this complexity is the demand for interactive systems that not only identify gem shapes from user-uploaded images but also recommend matching 3D jewelry designs and update those models in real time with the provided gem's appearance. Most existing solutions either operate in isolation—focusing on a single task such as gem classification—or lack the ability to dynamically update 3D models with realistic gem textures and customizable jewelry colors. This creates a critical research gap in integrating computer vision, machine learning, and real-time 3D rendering technologies into a unified, accessible platform capable of intelligent gem analysis, authenticity verification, and personalized jewelry visualization.

Proposed Solution

To address the identified challenges, this research proposes an AI-powered, end-to-end system that combines deep learning, image processing, and real-time 3D rendering to deliver a comprehensive solution for gemstone analysis and jewelry customization. The system will first utilize advanced computer vision techniques, such as Mask R-CNN, to accurately detect and segment gemstone regions from complex jewelry images, even when the gem is partially covered by metal or affected by environmental variations. To determine authenticity, the system will analyze visual and structural features—such as color patterns, surface inclusions, and internal reflections—using a trained convolutional neural network (CNN) to classify the gem as real or synthetic, without relying on specialized gemological equipment. After identifying the gem’s shape and characteristics, the platform will intelligently recommend compatible 3D jewelry designs from a predefined model library. The selected design will then be updated in real time by replacing the gem part with the uploaded gem image, ensuring a realistic match in terms of texture and appearance. Users will also be able to interactively change the color of the jewelry metal (e.g., gold, silver, rose gold) using an integrated UI, with updates reflected live in a 3D environment powered by Three.js and Blender. This unified solution enhances the gemstone selection and customization experience for users, gemologists, and online jewelry retailers by merging automated gem analysis with dynamic 3D visualization in a single, accessible web-based platform.

Research Objectives

Gem Identification

The first objective of this research is to develop a computer vision-based model capable of accurately identifying and segmenting gemstone components from non-gem parts in jewelry images. This includes handling complex scenarios where the gem is embedded in items such as rings, pendants, or earrings, and may be partially obscured by metal settings or photographed under diverse lighting conditions. The goal is to ensure high precision in gem detection to support further classification and design tasks.

01

Gem Authentication

The second objective is to design a deep learning-based classification system that can distinguish between real (natural) and synthetic gemstones using only image-based features. This involves extracting visual cues such as color consistency, surface texture, and internal inclusions, and training the model on a diverse dataset to improve reliability and accuracy. The aim is to provide an accessible and non-invasive alternative to traditional gem testing methods.

02

3D Jewelry Models

The third objective is to create an intelligent recommendation engine that identifies the shape of a gemstone from an uploaded image and suggests compatible 3D jewelry designs accordingly. Additionally, the system should dynamically replace the gem texture in the selected 3D model with the user’s gem image and allow real-time customization of jewelry metal colors. This objective focuses on enhancing personalization and interactivity in the jewelry design process using technologies like Three.js and Blender.

03

Customize Jewelry

The forth objective is implement algorithms such as edge detection and contour analysis to analyze multiple input measurements and determine the most accurate dimensions of the face. And develop an algorithm that suggests the most suitable jewelry options and jewelry customization features.
